Crawlr: HowTo

What is this?

Imagine that it's Friday evening -- you just finished a long week of work and are ready to paint the town red with your friends. Unfortunately, the group message you send out asking people what they would like to do is met with an overwhelming "I don't know". What do you do now?

In comes Crawlr!

Instead of spending the next 20 minutes arguing over what to do, let Crawlr plan your perfect night. All Crawlr needs to know is where you want to start, how far you want to go, what kinds of places you like, and how long you want to spend having the best night of your week

I'm Ready! What's First?

The first thing you need to know is where you want to start

This is a formatted address in the form of, for example, '1521 Pearl St, Boulder, CO'. This means that you should look up an address in the general area you want to visit ahead of time, and use that!

Why are we doing it this way? Wouldn't it just be easier for you to put in a location by name, or just ask for your current location from your phone?

Well, sort of...
We *don't* plan on this format being in the final app, but because this is a prototype, and because of some stuff going on behind the scenes, we really need that address (for now)

Ok, What's Next?

Now Crawlr needs to know how far you're willing to travel

You specify a distance which Crawlr uses as a radius to search for places you might like

Basically, imagine drawing a circle around your starting location, where the distance from your location to the edge of the circle is the distance you give

Any place Crawlr tells you to go will be inside that circle, meaning if you don't feel like walking far, Crawlr will plan around it!

But what if you want to go farther than the given options?

We would love for people to be able to go as far as they want -- eventually. Right now we are using Google to get data about places, and if we look too far then we use up all the data Google gives us for a day. In the full application, we hope to let people plan huge trips which can include driving and public transportation

What's This Budget Thing?

The first thing you're probably thinking when you see each budget entry is "well how much money is this?"

Our answer at the moment is "We have no idea!"

So why are we including this?

Well, think about it -- if you're looking for places to go eat on the internet, you probably are looking at the dollar sign ratings for each place.

That's what this is

By specifying that you want to, for example, have a budget of one dollar sign, Crawlr will make sure that the average dollar sign rating of your trip does not exceed one dollar sign.

This means that you have a little more flexibility in controlling how much you want to spend

So why don't we just ask you how much money you want to spend?

Because that's insanely hard

Like, really, really hard

We don't know what you're going to order at each place, and different dollar sign ratings can mean different things for different places. For example, five dollar signs at a coffee shop is probably a way different dollar amount than five dollar signs at a steak house

I Think I Understand the Time...

You probably do, but here's some important stuff to consider

1. If you specify too little time, Crawlr probably will tell you it can't find you a route
This is pretty straightforward -- if you don't have enough time to travel to any of the places around you, or actually spend time at any of them, then there isn't a route available

2. If you specify too much time, your results might not be what you expect
Right now, Crawlr only looks at places which are open when you request a route and stay open for the whole time you want to spend on a route. This means that you could request a route at 5:00 PM, and want to spend 6 hours on a route, and get no places back. This could happen if all the places Crawlr finds close before 11:00 PM, meaning it can't find any places to send you

I'm Ready to Go!

No, you're not

You still need to enter what kinds of places you'd like to go!

This is where you specify

1. What kinds of places you would like to visit
This tells Crawlr whether you'd like to go to cafes, bars, restaurants, or any of the options we've provided

2. How long you'd be willing to spend at an individual place
This tells crawlr the maximum amount of time you'd be willing to spend at any given place which falls under the corresponding category

3. How many places you'd be willing to visit
This is NOT a guarantee on the number of places you will visit. If you choose 'Unlimited', you are saying you don't care how many places of a specific type you'd be willing to visit, and this includes 0 places!

The same goes for 'No more than', with the exception that you specify an upper limit

For 'Exactly', keep in mind that if Crawlr can't find any places under the corresponding type, then it will say it can't find a route. This is because you asked for exactly a certain number of places, and Crawlr can't find that many places, therefore it can't find a route that you said you want!

For 'More than', it's the same as 'Exactly', just that Crawlr might choose more than the number of places you give

Ok, NOW You're Ready to Go!

Thanks so much for using Crawlr!

Keep in mind that this is still very much in development, and we are making changes every day to make the app better!

If you have any feedback for our application, PLEASE leave a comment in the feedback section once you receive your route -- we will be constantly monitoring feedback, so if you have anything you'd like to see added or updated, let us know!
